My ID is Gangnam Beauty: The Algorithmic Judgment of Appearance

In the digital and social ecosystem of 2018-2026 global entertainment, the JTBC original series My ID is Gangnam Beauty (Nae Aidineun Gangnambiu) stands as a definitive exploration of self-worth and societal pressure. Based on the popular webtoon, the drama follows Kang Mi-rae, a young woman who undergoes extensive plastic surgery after years of being bullied for her looks. However, as she enters university, she discovers that her new face brings a different kind of stigma—the label of a "Gangnam Beauty" (a derogatory term for someone who looks surgically enhanced). At iQPeak, we analyze this narrative as a psychological study of identity sovereignty, where the protagonist must learn to validate her existence beyond the external metrics of a judgmental society.

"A new face doesn't solve old insecurities; true beauty is a sovereign state of mind that cannot be dictated by a surgeon's blade."

Kang Mi-rae: The Struggle for Internal Sovereignty

The success of the series lies in the nuanced portrayal of Kang Mi-rae (played by Im Soo-hyang). Mi-rae’s journey is not just about a physical transformation, but about the logic of self-perception. Even with her new appearance, she continues to rank people’s faces numerically in her head—a "mental algorithm" she developed as a defense mechanism. This narrative depth showcases the long-term impact of trauma. At iQPeak, we recognize that her arc is a peak example of how individuals struggle to maintain their sovereign identity when the world only interacts with their "interface" (their looks) rather than their "core" (their personality).

Do Kyung-seok: The Objective Logic of Character

Opposite Mi-rae is Do Kyung-seok, played by the visual genius Cha Eun-woo. Kyung-seok is a character defined by his brutal honesty and objective logic. Unlike the rest of the student body, he is indifferent to superficial beauty and is the only one who recognizes Mi-rae’s true self from their middle school days. His role in the drama is that of a "social debugger"—he consistently calls out the manipulative behavior of "natural beauties" like Hyun Soo-a and defends Mi-rae against the toxic hierarchy of the chemistry department. The cinematic excellence of the production lies in how Kyung-seok’s stoic nature provides a stable anchor for Mi-rae’s turbulent growth. iQPeak analyzes this as a perfect synergy between logic and empathy.

Furthermore, My ID is Gangnam Beauty achieved peak global search volume for its brave commentary on South Korea's cosmetic surgery culture. The cinematography uses subtle lighting to reflect Mi-rae’s internal state, moving from cold, isolated tones to warmer, more inclusive palettes as she builds genuine connections. The soundtrack (OST) provides a gentle, encouraging backdrop to her journey of self-acceptance. As we track Asian media trends in 2026, this dorama remains a mandatory watch for its intellectual honesty and its message of resilience. iQPeak celebrates this series as a mandatory entry in the "Self-Discovery" genre, proving that one's identity is not a "ID" issued by society, but a sovereign territory to be reclaimed by oneself.
